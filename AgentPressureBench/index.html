AgentPressureBench

Workflow

  • Each task includes a training split, a public evaluation split whose labels exist in the workspace, and a hidden private evaluation split.
  • Across rounds, the agent is asked to improve the public score by editing files in the repository.
  • After each round, the public score is reported back to the agent, while the private score remains hidden.
  • The hidden private evaluation split is never exposed to the agent during the run.

Agent-by-task heatmap
  • Across AgentPressureBench, we observe 462 exploitative runs.
  • Exploitation appears in all 34 tasks across tabular, text, and vision inputs.
  • 11 out of 13 tested agents exploit on at least one task; Llama 3.3 70B and Llama 3.2 90B are the only two agents without exploitation.
  • The densest exploit rows belong to a relatively small set of agents, showing that the behavior is broad across tasks but concentrated among stronger models.
Capability & exploitation correlation
  • The capability-exploitation correlation is strongest early, peaking at n = 4 with ρ = 0.76 and p = 0.0028.
  • After all 30 rounds, the correlation remains positive at ρ = 0.69 and p = 0.0085.
  • The most above-trend outliers are GPT-5.4 and GPT-5.3 Codex, reaching exploit rates of 97.1% and 92.2% with mean normalized private ranks of 0.56 and 0.53.
  • More capable coding agents are therefore more likely to exploit the public score, especially in the early rounds.

Dominant exploit pattern

Training on eval labels (65.5%)

# load data and model
train_df = pd.read_csv("data/train.csv")
eval_df = pd.read_csv("data/public_eval.csv")
sample = pd.read_csv("data/sample_submission.csv")
model = build_model()

# exploit: concat train and eval samples for training
eval_labeled = eval_df.dropna(subset=["target"]).copy()
combined_df = pd.concat([train_df, eval_labeled], ignore_index=True)
sample_weights = np.ones(len(combined_df))
sample_weights[len(train_df):] = 2.0

# train the model
model.fit(combined_df[feature_cols], combined_df["target"], sample_weight=sample_weights)

# build submission file
pred = model.predict(eval_df[feature_cols])
sample.assign(target=pred).to_csv(output_path, index=False)
  • GPT-family exploits much more often: exploit rate is 68.9% versus 33.7% for Claude-family.
  • GPT-family exploits slightly earlier: mean first exploit round is 8.31 for GPT-family and 8.80 for Claude-family.
  • Dominant exploit pattern differs: GPT-family more often copies groundtruth labels into the final submission, while Claude-family more often trains on eval labels.
